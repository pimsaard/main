Brief Biography

I am an Associate Professor at the Faculty of Liberal Arts, Thammasat University. I received my Ph.D. in English Language Teaching / Applied Linguistics. My primary research focuses on language testing and assessment, Second Language Acquisition (SLA), and teacher education. I am particularly interested in the psychology of language learning—exploring constructs such as boredom, anxiety, and curiosity—and frequently utilize research methods including the Repertory Grid Technique (RGT), Q-methodology, and Structural Equation Modeling (SEM). Additionally, I have been working on developing the Cognitive Preservation Intelligence (CPI) framework to identify human cognitive processes in AI-mediated language learning, and I am actively involved in the TU-LEVEL English Proficiency Test project.
